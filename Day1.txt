Measuring Efficiency: Big O Notation
Big O notation is the language and metric used to describe the efficiency of algorithms, especially as input sizes grow
. It helps standardise how we express an algorithm's behaviour (e.g., how it performs with 10 pages versus 1,000,000 pages of data)
.
Complexity Types:
Time Complexity: Measures the time an algorithm takes to execute (e.g., Code 1 taking 30 seconds vs. Code 2 taking 60 seconds)
.
Space Complexity: Measures the amount of memory an algorithm uses
.
Performance Cases: Algorithms are evaluated based on their Best case, Average case, and Worst case scenarios
. These scenarios are often compared to real-world analogies, such as fuel consumption in city traffic versus highway driving
.
